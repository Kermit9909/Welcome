
## Wireless LANs - 802.11 standard


* CSMA/CD is used in wired networks to **detect and recover from collisions**
* CSMA/CA is used in wireless networks to **avoid collisions**

Wireless Considerations

	- Absorption
	- Relflection
	- Refraction
	- Diffraction "blind spot"
	- Scattering "uneven surface, smog, etc"
	- Interference "other devices using same channel"

## Radio Frequency

* Range from 30 Hz to 300 hz
* Wireless LANs use:
	* 2.4 ghz -Best channels ( 1, 6, 11) further reach in open space and better penetration of obstacles such as walls 
	* 5 ghz - Non-overlapping channels (more channels for bandwidth)

## Service Sets

	*Independent
	*Infrastructure
	*Mesh*

	-IBSS (Independent Basic Sercie Set) is a wireless network in which two or more wireless devices connect directly **without using** an AP
	- Also called an "ad hoc"
	- File transfer (Air Drop)

	-BSS (Infrastructure) Basic Service Set in which clients connect to each other via an AP
	- BSSID is used to uniquely id the AP ( basically the MAC address of the AP)
	- BSA (Basic Service Area) the area around the BSSID

	-ESS (Extended Service Set)
	- This is what allows Roaming
